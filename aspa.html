Types of Aptitude Questions

Quantitative Aptitude:

  • Basic arithmetic operations: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division.
  • Percentage, profit and loss, ratio and proportion.
  • Time and distance, time and work, average, mixture and alligation.
  • Problems on ages, numbers, and series.
  • Data interpretation: tables, charts, graphs.

Logical Reasoning:

  • Series completion: number, letter, and mixed series.
  • Coding-decoding: coding and decoding of words, symbols, patterns.
  • Blood relations, directions, seating arrangement, puzzles.
  • Syllogism, logical deductions.
  • Analogies, classification, logical sequencing.

Verbal Ability:

  • Reading comprehension: understanding passages and answering questions based on them.
  • Vocabulary: synonyms, antonyms, word meanings, idioms, phrases.
  • Grammar: sentence correction, fill in the blanks, error spotting.
  • Para jumbles: rearranging sentences to form a coherent paragraph.
  • Critical reasoning: drawing inferences, evaluating arguments.

Numerical Ability:

  • Number series, missing numbers, odd one out.
  • Number system: divisibility, factors, multiples, prime numbers.
  • Decimal fractions, simplification, approximation.
  • Square roots, cube roots, exponentiation.
  • Permutations and combinations, probability.

Data Interpretation:

  • Tabular data interpretation: extracting information from tables and solving related questions.
  • Bar graphs, pie charts, line graphs, histograms.
  • Data sufficiency: determining if given data is sufficient to answer a question.
  • Caselet data interpretation: interpreting data presented in the form of a case study.

Analytical Ability:

  • Pattern recognition: identifying patterns and applying them to solve problems.
  • Problem-solving: tackling complex problems using logical reasoning and analytical skills.
  • Critical thinking: evaluating information, drawing conclusions, and making decisions.
  • Mathematical puzzles and brain teasers.
