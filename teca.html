SCHLAHUB

Tech Mahindra Interview Questions

Technical Questions:

Software Development:

  • Explain the difference between procedural programming and object-oriented programming.
  • What is polymorphism? Provide an example.
  • Describe the concept of inheritance in object-oriented programming.
  • Explain the difference between method overloading and method overriding.
  • What is a pointer in C? How is it different from a reference in C++?

Data Structures and Algorithms:

  • Explain the concept of time complexity and space complexity.
  • Describe the working principle of binary search and its time complexity.
  • What is a linked list? Explain the difference between a singly linked list and a doubly linked list.
  • Discuss different sorting algorithms and their time complexities.
  • What is a hash table? Explain its implementation and applications.

Database Management:

  • What is normalization and denormalization in databases?
  • Describe the ACID properties of a transaction.
  • Explain the difference between primary key, unique key, and foreign key.
  • What are the different types of joins in SQL? Provide examples.
  • How do you optimize database queries for better performance?

Web Development:

  • Describe the difference between HTTP and HTTPS.
  • Explain the concept of RESTful APIs.
  • What is the purpose of CSS preprocessors like SASS and LESS?
  • How do you handle cross-browser compatibility issues in web development?
  • What are some best practices for improving website performance?

Behavioral Questions:

Teamwork and Collaboration:

  • Describe a situation where you had to work as part of a team to achieve a common goal.
  • How do you handle disagreements or conflicts within a team?
  • Share an example of a successful project collaboration experience.

Problem Solving:

  • Describe a challenging technical problem you faced and how you solved it.
  • How do you approach solving complex technical problems?
  • Share an example of a time when you had to quickly learn a new technology or programming language to complete a project.

Communication Skills:

  • How do you communicate technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders?
  • Describe a situation where you had to explain a technical issue to someone with limited technical knowledge.
  • Share an example of a time when your communication skills helped resolve a misunderstanding or conflict.

Adaptability:

  • Describe a situation where you had to adapt to a change in project requirements or deadlines.
  • How do you stay updated with the latest technologies and trends in your field?
  • Share an example of a time when you successfully adapted to a new work environment or team dynamic.

Data Sufficiency:

  • Determine whether the information provided in two statements is sufficient to answer a given question.
    • Example:
      Statement 1: x > 5
      Statement 2: x < 10
      Is x an even number?

Probability and Permutation/Combination:

  • Calculate the probability of certain events occurring.
  • Solve problems involving arrangements and selections of objects.
    • Example:
      What is the probability of rolling a prime number with a fair six-sided die?
      How many different 3-letter combinations can be formed from the letters A, B, C, D without repetition?

Time, Speed, and Distance:

  • Solve problems involving relative speeds, time taken to cover distances, and average speeds.
    • Example:
      If a car travels at a speed of 60 km/h for the first hour, 50 km/h for the second hour, and 40 km/h for the third hour, what is the average speed for the entire journey?

Ratio and Proportion:

  • Solve problems involving proportions and ratios.
    • Example:
      If the ratio of the number of boys to girls in a class is 3:5 and there are 24 boys, how many girls are there?

Algebraic Equations:

  • Solve equations involving algebraic expressions, linear equations, and quadratic equations.
    • Example:
      Solve for x: 2x + 5 = 15

Geometry:

  • Solve problems involving geometric figures, such as triangles, circles, squares, and rectangles.
    • Example:
      Calculate the area of a circle with radius 5 cm.

Number Series:

  • Identify patterns in number series and find the missing number.
    • Example:
      2, 5, 10, 17, ?

Age Problems:

  • Solve problems involving the ages of people at different points in time, considering factors like birth, events, and relationships.
    • Example:
      A father is four times as old as his son. In 20 years, he will be twice as old as his son. What are their current ages?

Percentage Problems:

  • Solve problems involving percentages, such as percentage increase/decrease, profit/loss percentage, and discounts.
    • Example:
      If the price of a product is reduced by 20%, by what percentage should it be increased to get back to its original price?
